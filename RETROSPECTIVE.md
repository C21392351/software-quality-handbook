## Retrospective

## What went well

- The use of feature branches and pull requests ensured that we did not have any conflicts or problems during the work process
- Reviewing the content regularly contributed towards maintaining high quality and consistency in our handbook
- The research sources mainly focused on real developer experiences which made our content more relevant
- All members of the team communicated efficiently when adjustments were necessary

## What didn't go so well

- There was some initial difficulties with the first pull request as it was merged without being reviewed but was later fixed
- Some inconsistent file structure where there was files created at the root directory instead of the sections directory
- Small formatting inconsistencies among team members that needed correcting later on
- Some overlapping information between research sources

## What we would do differently next time 

- Defining the structure and naming clearly from the very start
- Clearly agreeing on a set format before writing content
- Scheduling check-ins more frequently or hold small meetings to stay coordinated
- Use a shared checklist so it is clearer to see the progress made by team members and what stage they are at
- Encourage more detailed feedback on some pull requests
